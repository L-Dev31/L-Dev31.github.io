As of today, I can no longer say that I am a beginner in SMG 3D modeling. I see myself polishing models so intensively that someone who hasn't played SMG or SMG2 in a long time couldn't tell my models weren't official Nintendo creations.


But it clearly wasn't a one day adventure.


I personally think that if I made it to this point, it's largely due to my exaggerated perfectionism which forced me to never use anything unless I could call it "parfait en tout point" (perfect in every way). This, I assume, made SMAG's development so lengthy that it could be a AAA game today if it wasn't a mod made by fanboys/nerds like us, and especially if we already knew everything we know today (and more...).


With this long introduction, let's now look at the advancement of my 3D modeling journey...

First Real Model: The Blender Breakthrough

Then here we go with my first ever 3D model made on Blender. If I'd open it today and examine it, I'd be deeply horrified, this model had a ton of wasted resources (overly duplicated faces, etc.) but it already looked like something, I must say.


I've got to be honest: it is this model that made me want to start SMAG. When I looked at it, it was so good compared to everything else I had made that I thought this model alone could beat any of Louis Miles' work! (laughs) Also, this is thanks to Alex SMG that I managed to make that model and develop a passion for 3D modeling with his "How to import a model in SMG" tutorial that featured a quick session on Blender.



Oh, and let's not forget FRANCE!



Phase 1: The Copying Phase, Learning from the Masters

This is where it really gets interesting. This was the time I had only one thing in mind: "be better than any other SMG 3D modeler."


Not gonna lie, this is not really possible, each one of us has widely different styles, and I couldn't name a #1 modeler among all of us, except maybe Louis Miles, who has a style that I truly adore and love to analyze before starting a new model.


Let's get back to the topic... In this period, it was simple for me: I was copying every model I looked at. I remember copying:

  • Kairos' circular shaped planet
  • Into the Gateway planet from SMG for the spherical one
  • SMGravity demo's first planet for the odd shaped one
  • Gusty Garden's first planet for the UFO shaped model
  • SMG2's first planet for the planet with fences

Well, this took me 5 years of my small life, but looking at these results, I can say it was completely worth it. Current SMAG models showcase the culmination of this entire journey, from those early SketchUp experiments to professional-grade 3D artistry that rivals official Nintendo work (at the very least from back then).


Conclusion: From Dreams to Reality

From those humble and sometimes questionable beginnings, every mistake taught me something valuable. Every copied model helped me understand geometry better. Every sleepless night spent modeling built my passion and determination.


Today, looking back at this journey, I can say that perfectionism, while sometimes slowing down development, pushed me to constantly improve and never settle for "good enough."


The canceled models you see here weren't failures, they were stepping stones. Each one brought me closer to the level of quality you see in SMAG today.


The lesson? Every expert was once a beginner. Every professional was once an amateur. The key is to never stop learning, never stop improving, and always remember where you came from.


Thank you for following this journey with me. Here's to many more years of creating amazing 3D worlds for everyone to enjoy!
