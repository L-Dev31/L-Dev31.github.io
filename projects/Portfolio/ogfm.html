The Brief

As a Graphic Design and Project Management student at Sophia Ynov Campus, this project was assigned by our teacher Mr. Loïc Swiny. The client: OG FM, a nostalgia-driven DIY radio whose motto says it all “Pour les jeunes cons qui sont devenus vieux” (For the young fools who grew old). A station for those who refuse to let the golden years of music disappear quietly. Their identity needed to feel exactly like that: urgent, physical, unapologetic.

Punk isn’t the absence of a system. It’s a system that refuses everyone else’s.

The Problem

The Former Logo

Their original logo was clean, balanced, forgettable. Nothing about it said dangerous. It looked like every other webradio and conveyed none of what they actually stood for. The brief wrote itself: burn it down and build something that couldn’t be mistaken for anything else.

The Process

Finding the DNA

UK DIY and fanzine culture were the answer. But every wannabe punk brand borrows that aesthetic as a costume. The question was how to make it undeniably real, not referential.

Getting to It

If every wannabe punk brand fakes the aesthetic in Photoshop, the answer was to not use Photoshop at all. I picked up a ballpoint pen and drew it on old paper. Cut it out with bare hands. No grid, no undo, no control. The imperfections weren’t a style choice. They were proof.

Into Illustrator

Illustrator wasn’t there to clean anything up. I scanned the mark raw and vectorized it directly, trembling lines and all. The goal was to make the chaos permanent. Then the logotype: I broke every rule I knew. Two, three fonts maximum, they say. I used seven. Each one technically wrong, each one forced into coexistence through sheer stubbornness. Stretched, cut, offset until they stopped fighting. Seven fonts. One identity.

Realising It

With the mark locked, the identity had to hold across everything: mockups, affiches, Instagram posts and stories. Every touchpoint had to carry the same DNA without copying it. The rules set in the logo became the filter for every decision. Then came the real test: the OQG 20th anniversary. A milestone that had to feel earned, not decorated. The system held.

A strong identity isn’t beautiful. It’s immediately recognizable.

What It Left Me With

First graphic charter. First real brand identity. A lot of mistakes and even more learnings. The thing that stayed: consistency is what turns a style into a brand. The rules don’t kill the punk. They protect it.
