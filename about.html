The story

Childhood

My foundation was tactile before it was digital. I grew up creating with clay, crayons, and sketchbooks in Provence.
Later, I taught myself GameMaker, started publishing videos, and explored modding, which naturally pushed me toward UI/UX and 3D.

Guadeloupe

At 15, I moved to Guadeloupe and built in real conditions, across cultures and constraints. I learned Kreyol Gwadloup and launched Rayon, my first marketplace project. That chapter taught me to think end to end: product, design, and execution.

Now

Today, I am a student refining my craft while working with agencies and brands. I keep a T-shaped perspective: design at the core, with curiosity in code, politics, history, and culture. I build digital work that is clear, ambitious, and grounded in real use.
